\title{On long $\kappa$-tuples with few prime factors}

\author{O. Ramar\'e}
%\date{\sl January, the 7th of 2004}

%\classno{11N35, 11N36 (primary), 42B10 (secondary)}

%\date{Received: date / Accepted: date}

\begin{document}
%\email{ramare@math.univ-lille1.fr}
%\keywords{Weighted sieve, Selberg sieve, Prime $\kappa$-tuple}

  We prove that there are infinitely many
  integers $n$ such that the total number of prime factors of
  $(n+h_1)\cdots(n+h_\kappa)$ is exactly $(1+o(1))\kappa\Log\kappa$. Our
  result even ensures us that these prime factors are fairly evenly
  distributed among every factors $n+h_i$.
